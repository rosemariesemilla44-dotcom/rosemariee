Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun, known for its extreme temperatures and unique orbital characteristics.

Size and Composition: Mercury has a radius of about 2,440 km (1,516 miles), making it slightly larger than Earth's Moon. It is a terrestrial planet, primarily composed of rock and metal, with a thin silicate crust and a large iron core that constitutes about 60% of its mass.

Orbit and Rotation: Mercury orbits the Sun at an average distance of 58 million km (36 million miles) and completes a revolution in just 88 Earth days, making it the fastest planet in the solar system. Its rotation period is about 59 Earth days, resulting in a unique day-night cycle where one solar day lasts approximately 176 Earth days.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun, often called Earth's "twin" due to its similar size and composition, but it has extreme surface conditions and a dense, toxic atmosphere.

Size and Composition: Venus orbits the Sun at an average distance of approximately 108 million kilometers (67 million miles). It has a radius of 6,052 kilometers (3,760 miles) and a gravity about 90% that of Earth.

Rotation and Orbit: Venus takes about 243 Earth days to complete one rotation, and it rotates in the opposite direction to most planets. A Venusian day lasts nearly 117 Earth days. Its orbit around the Sun takes 225 Earth days.

Earth

Earth, third planet from the Sun and the fifth largest planet in the solar system. Its single most outstanding feature is that its near-surface environments are the only places in the universe known to harbour life.

Size and Composition: Earth is the only planet in our solar system with liquid water on the surface.

Rotation and Orbit: Earth rotates on its axis approximately every 24 hours and orbits the Sun once every 365.25 days, influencing day-night cycles and seasonal changes.

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is known as a dusty, cold desert world with a very thin atmosphere. It is the second smallest planet in the solar system, only larger than Mercury.

Size and Composition: Mars has a diameter of approximately 6,779 km (4,212 miles), making it about half the size of Earth. Its core is made of iron, nickel, and sulfur, surrounded by a rocky mantle and a crust composed of iron, magnesium, aluminum, calcium, and potassium.

Rotation and Orbit: Mars orbits the Sun every 687 Earth days and rotates on its axis approximately every 24 hours and 37 minutes.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system and the fifth planet from the Sun. It is a gas giant, primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, with a mass nearly 2.5 times that of all the other planets combined.

Size and Composition: Jupiter has a diameter of about 142,984 kilometers and a mass that is 318 times that of Earth.

Rotation and Orbit: Jupiter's rotation takes about 9.9 hours to complete one full rotation on its axis — the fastest of all planets.

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun, a gas giant known for its extensive ring system and numerous moons.

Size and Composition: Saturn has an equatorial diameter of about 120,500 kilometers (74,897 miles), making it 9 times wider than Earth. It is primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, with traces of ammonia, methane, and water ices.

Rotation and Orbit: Saturn's equatorial region rotates in approximately 10 hours 14 minutes, while higher latitudes rotate in about 10 hours 39 minutes.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun, known for its unique tilt, icy composition, and faint rings, making it one of the most intriguing planets in our solar system.

Size and Composition: Uranus has an equatorial diameter of approximately 51,118 kilometers, making it four times wider than Earth. It is primarily composed of water, ammonia, and methane in a supercritical phase, often referred to as ice.

Rotation and Orbit: Uranus has a rotational period of approximately 17 hours and 14 minutes and an orbital period of about 84 Earth years.

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun, known for its striking blue color and powerful winds, making it a fascinating ice giant in our solar system.

Size and Composition: Neptune has a diameter of approximately 49,244 kilometers (30,598 miles) and a mass 17 times that of Earth, primarily composed of hydrogen, helium, water, ammonia, and methane.

Rotation and Orbit: Neptune completes its orbit around the Sun in about 165 Earth years and has a rotation period of approximately 16.11 hours.
